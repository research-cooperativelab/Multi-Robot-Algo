02  /  Research Question
The question is not whether.
03 / 13
How do you coordinate a small fleet of battery-limited robots to find a target hidden among n sites — without knowing where it is?
The Setup
  • R   robots, finite energy E
  • n   candidate sites in a search area
  • p   prior probability per site (sensor data)
  • τ   one hidden target, drawn from p
Our Goal
Find an assignment & order that minimizes the finding robot’s travel.

03  /  Our Metric
Finder Competitive Ratio
04 / 13
FCR =  Df Dopt
Df distance the finding robot actually traveled.
Dopt distance an omniscient agent would have flown.
FCR = 1  →  the finder flew the geodesic. Anything above is overhead from uncertainty.
Lower FCR = better coordination.

06  /  How M4★ Runs
Auction, then chain.
07 / 13
1
SSI Auction.
Each robot bids on every feasible site. Highest p/d² wins. Repeat until every robot has an anchor.
2
Greedy Chain.
From the anchor, append the next-best feasible site as long as the round-trip fits in remaining energy. ~5 sites per sortie.
3
Bayesian Update.
Robots return to base, broadcast results, zero-out visited priors, renormalize, then re-auction.

07  /  Key Insight
Why p/d²?
08 / 13
Cost foreclosure.
Anchoring at distance d burns 2d of the budget on base legs — leaving E−2d for chain extensions. Distant anchors aren’t just expensive; they foreclose on future hops.
Classic rule (1975)
p / d
Linear penalty. Works for a single searcher with unlimited battery.
This work
p / d²
Quadratic penalty captures foreclosure under finite energy.
Mean FCR by bid function · 5,000 trials.

12  /  Closing
Four takeaways.
13 / 13
01
Communication beats fleet size.
The dominant lever is whether robots talk — not how many you launch. 6.6× gain from coordination alone.
02
Chain trips, not bigger batteries.
Multi-node sorties recover 90% of the energy penalty — in software, no hardware changes.
03
p/d² is the right bid under finite E.
Advantage grows under tight budgets and noisy maps — exactly when it matters most in real SAR.
04
All open-source & live.
