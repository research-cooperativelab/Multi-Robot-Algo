\textbf{ABSTRACT}

\vspace{0.25in}
\textbf{ON THE NUMERICAL ANALYSIS OF MULTI-ROBOT SEARCH\\
AND RESCUE ALGORITHMS IN UNKNOWN\\
ENVIRONMENTS}

\vspace{0.25in}
By

\vspace{0.1in}
Fozhan Babaeiyan Ghamsari

\vspace{0.1in}
Spring 2026
\end{center}

\begin{doublespacing}
\noindent
This thesis studies the multi-robot search and rescue problem in which
$R$ robots must locate a target hidden among $n$ candidate sites, each
carrying a prior probability $p_i$ of containing the target, under a
finite per-robot energy budget $E$. Four algorithmic models that
systematically vary coordination and energy assumptions are formulated
and evaluated by the Finder Competitive Ratio (FCR), the ratio of the
finding robot's travel distance to the omniscient optimum. Theoretical
upper bounds are derived for each model and validated over 500 Monte
Carlo trials with parameter sweeps. Auction-based coordination yields
a 6.6-fold improvement over random search; a bid function $p_i/d^2$
that penalizes distance quadratically outperforms the standard $p_i/d$
by 4.2 percent under energy constraints, motivated by a
cost-foreclosure argument; and multi-node sorties close 92 percent of
the gap between energy-constrained and unconstrained performance.
\end{doublespacing}
